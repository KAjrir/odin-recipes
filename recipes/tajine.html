Moroccan Tajine

Description

A Moroccan tajine is a traditional North African dish named after the clay pot it's cooked in. It consists of slow-cooked stews made with meat, vegetables, and aromatic spices like saffron, cinnamon, and cumin. The unique pot design locks in moisture, creating rich, flavorful dishes.

Ingredients

  • Meat: 500g lamb, chicken, or beef
  • egetables: Carrots, potatoes, zucchini, onions, tomatoes
  • Spices: 1 tsp ground cumin, 1 tsp ground cinnamon, 1 tsp paprika, 1/2 tsp ground ginger, pinch of saffron threads, salt, and black pepper
  • Herbs: Fresh cilantro and parsley (chopped)
  • Fruits: Handful of dried apricots, prunes, or raisins
  • Olive oil: 2-3 tbsp
  • Garlic: 2-3 cloves, minced
  • Stock or water: 1 cup (250 ml)
  • Garnish: Toasted almonds or sesame seeds
  • Preserved lemons: 1, thinly sliced
  • Green or black olives: Handful, for extra flavor

Steps

  1. Prepare the ingredients: Chop vegetables, mince garlic, and season the meat (if using) with salt, pepper, and a pinch of spices.
  2. Heat the tajine or pot: Add olive oil and saut√© onions and garlic until softened.
  3. Add the meat and spices: Brown the meat (if using) and stir in cumin, cinnamon, paprika, ginger, and saffron.
  4. Layer vegetables: Arrange carrots, potatoes, zucchini, and tomatoes over the meat.
  5. Add liquid: Pour in 1 cup of stock or water, and add preserved lemon slices and olives if desired.
  6. Simmer gently: Cover and cook on low heat for 1.5-2 hours (or until tender), occasionally checking the liquid level.
  7. Garnish and serve: Top with fresh cilantro, parsley, and optional toasted almonds or sesame seeds. Serve with warm bread or couscous.
